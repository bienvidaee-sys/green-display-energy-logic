Open-Source Energy saving logic for public displays. A sensor-based approach to replace 24hrs consumption and save energy



Millions of public advertising displays operate 24/7, consuming massive amounts of energy even when no one is present. 
This is a legacy "steam engine" approach to infrastructure that results in enormous unnecessary waste and carbon emissions.
​The Solution:
Instead of hardware overhauls, I propose a simple, non-intrusive "Plug-and-Play" sensor-based logic.
​Passive Detection: Use a low-cost motion sensor to detect human presence.
​Soft-Start Mechanism: When someone is detected, the system executes a "Soft-Start" to avoid electrical surges, protecting the hardware while waking the display.
​Intelligent Standby: When no motion is detected for a specific period (e.g., 5 minutes), the display enters a low-power standby mode, not a hard power-off.
​The Impact (Simulation Result):
Based on standard 500W public displays, this logic can reduce annual energy consumption by approximately 60%, saving significant costs and reducing waste.
​Call to Action:
This is not a technical challenge; it is a choice to stop wasting. I am sharing this logic for engineers and innovators to implement. If we stop burning energy for empty streets, we can make a real difference.
